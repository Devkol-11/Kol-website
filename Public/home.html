Life as a Young Adult

"I hope you find the courage to grow out of what no longer serves you and into what gives you life." — Morgan Harper Nichols

Being a young adult can feel like a rollercoaster—exciting at times but also overwhelming and frustrating. I often think to myself, “Why isn’t there a guidebook for this stage of life? It would make things so much easier.”

We often find ourselves stuck in a whirlwind of distractions. We do things we know we shouldn’t, avoiding the real efforts that could make a difference. Deep down, we all have a picture of who we want to become—our “ideal self.”
But when things don’t go as planned, that vision can feel impossibly far away. This gap between where we are and where we want to be creates doubt and fear. Questions like, “What if I never become the person I dream of being? What if my goals are always out of reach?” can haunt us.

For me, understanding this phase of life has come from observing others who are ahead of me—family members, mentors, or people I admire.
While this might not work for everyone, it’s been a key part of my growth.

Let me share five crucial lessons I’ve learned from studying these people.

  1. 1 - Have Something You’re Actively Working Towards

    You’ve probably heard this a million times—I have too—maybe from our parents or the countless motivational reels we scroll through on social media daily.

    But really, and I mean really, if we pause for a moment and think about it, this is the ignition that kickstarts our life. It’s what propels us on our unique journey to get to where we want to be.

    There has to be something that truly drives us at this stage of life, something that excites us no matter when or where we do it. I’ve come to realize that having this purpose opens the door to achieving our desires—whether financial or otherwise.

    On the other hand, not having this leaves us feeling empty, ordinary, and directionless.

  2. 2. Do Things For You , Not For Others

    "To be yourself in a world that is constantly trying to make you something else is the greatest accomplishment." - Ralph Waldo Emerson

    The expectations of others—whether from family, friends, or even online strangers—often pressure us to tailor our decisions to gain their approval, which subconsciously leads us to do less for ourselves.

    People's expectations of us are constantly shifting. If we focus on chasing them, we will find ourselves trapped in an endless loop of validation-seeking mentality.

    While we have different responsibilities to our family, friends, and loved ones—especially those who have invested so much in us—we also have an important responsibility to ourselves. Sometimes, we focus so much on meeting the expectations of these people that we become oblivious to what we truly want for ourselves. Many of us never stop to ask, "What do I really want?"

    That being said, we have to step back and make a decision—not to ignore the world, but to stop living for it. And trust that being true to ourselves is more than enough.

  3. 3. Everything Worth Doing At The Moment , Is Worth Doing Well

    A few years ago, when I was a young boy, I remember my dad always telling me, “How you do anything is how you do everything.” At the time, it didn’t resonate with me, but as I’ve grown older, I’ve come to understand its meaning more deeply.

    At this stage of our lives, it’s easy to get rushed or distracted by the world around us. Often, we overlook the value of giving our best to what we’re doing in the moment. Later, we might look back and think, “I should have done this better when I had the chance.”

    Many of us have memories like this from our teenage years—moments of regret for the lack of effort we put into things that mattered. But here’s the good news: we’re still young adults, and we have the chance to learn from those mistakes. We now know better than to approach important tasks with a lackadaisical attitude.

    Whether it’s academics, learning a skill, or working toward a goal, we never truly know how these efforts will shape our future. That’s why, whatever you’re working on—no matter how small or big—approach it with heart, care, and effort.

    We have a responsibility to our future selves to always do our best now.

  4. 4. Just Do It

    At this stage of our lives, our dreams can be big and bold, but our doubts can often feel even bigger. Our hesitation often comes from a fear of rejection because we might feel unready or not good enough. But come to think of it—do we really need to have it all figured out at this stage of our lives? Just a few years ago, we were naive teenagers trying to navigate the world.

    How about this—we just do it (yeah, like the Nike slogan, lol). Messy, scared, or unready—JUST DO IT. We miss 100% of the shots we never take, and the real regret always comes from the things we didn’t try. The actions we take will always beat our hesitation.

    The perfect moment is a myth. We don’t always have to be ready; we just have to be bold enough.

    So, whether it’s a career move, a creative project, or maybe telling that girl or boy we like how we feel, let’s just go for it. We never really know how well things might turn out.

    “In the end, we only regret the chances we didn’t take, the relationships we were too afraid to have, and the decisions we waited too long to make.” — Lewis Carroll

  5. 5. Prioritize meaningful relationships

    Real relationships—those meaningful connections—are what help us become better people. At this stage of our lives, we need to realize that success isn’t just about money or what we can achieve (as important as those things are). It’s also about the people we surround ourselves with—family who supports us, and friends with similar mindsets who challenge us and keep us grounded.

    It’s not about how many friends you have. There’s nothing to brag about by having a long list of friends; there’s no award for that. What matters is prioritizing the people who make us better and continuing to build those relationships.

    We don’t have to do it alone; no one can. Those relationships are worth every effort we put into them, so it's very important that we try to find time for them. In today’s Nigerian society, it’s easy to get lost in meaningless relationships just for the sake of it or just to fit in.

    Take a step back and look around for the real ones—those who make us better and make life meaningful. Whether it’s close friends or romantic partners, these are the people we need to invest our time and effort in. Never take them for granted.
